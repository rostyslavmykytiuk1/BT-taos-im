Goal: replicate and **beat** the dominant competitor (one operator at IP 38.127.44.98, ~41–57 uids, owns 10/10 of top‑10) with a robust "warehouse-and-realize-winners" agent, and **stay top as long as possible** (emission at top covers re-registration; recycle uids when they decay).

## 0. Verified scoring ground truth (read first — several "obvious" assumptions are WRONG)

- **Score = `0.79·kappa_score + 0.21·pnl_score`. BOTH from REALIZED round‑trip PnL only.** Unrealized / inventory / mark‑to‑market is **never** scored. (`reward.py`)
- **Realized PnL is FIFO, net of both fee legs.** Each SELL matches the **oldest** open buy lot: `realized = (sell_px − oldest_buy_px)·qty − open_fee − close_fee`. Selling below the oldest lot records a **negative**; not selling records nothing. (`validator.py:3712‑3743`)
- **The realized matrix is DENSE** — the validator writes an empty `{}` column for every uid every step (`validator.py:3949‑3951`), so kappa's per‑book mean divides across **~2160 columns** over the 10800 s lookback. Measured consequences (ran real `kappa_3`):
  - Any positive book that clears the gate **floors at ~0.50 normalized** (3 obs → 0.5025).
  - **Density is a weak lever:** 3→0.50, 90→0.524, 200→0.541, 720→0.596, 1500→0.657. → don't over‑trade.
  - **The real lever is BREADTH** — maximize the *count* of books clearing the gate (each adds ~0.50+ to the median). Target **88–96 active positive books**.
- **A single realized loss is NOT catastrophic** (measured): one −0.5 among 720 positives → norm **1.0**; one −5.0 → 0.585; five −2.0 → 0.62. The real killer (prior live A/B: −0.0156) was **many** losses moving the **median**. → invariant is **"keep the median book positive,"** not "zero negatives ever." Small bounded losses are a usable tool.
- **Per‑book gate:** uid must be ≥ `min_lookback = 5400 sim‑s` old (auto‑satisfied from registration via the dense columns) AND the book needs **≥3 nonzero realized obs** in the trailing `lookback = 10800 sim‑s`. (`kappa.py`)
- **Inactive‑book cliff:** up to `int(0.375·128)=48` books with `kappa=None` are **free**; beyond 48 they score 0.0 and drag the median. (`reward.py`)
- **Activity factor:** `activity.impact=0` ⇒ an active book = exactly 1.0 (volume gives **no** boost); a book with **no** closed RT within the rolling `sampling_interval=600 sim‑s` decays below 1.0 (haircut). → need ≥1 closed RT/book/≤600 s to avoid decay. (`reward.py:185‑188`)
- **Hard volume cap:** PLACE rejected once a book's rolling‑24 h‑sim volume ≥ `capital_turnover_cap·wealth = 10·50000 = 500,000` (CANCELs exempt). (`query.py:238`) We run ~45–90k → far under.
- **`pnl_score` (0.21 weight) is realized‑based** (`median_daily_return/2`), a small positive bonus if books net positive daily. (Separate from the off `kappa.pnl.impact=0`.)
- **Sim facts:** 128 books, price ~300, **tick 0.01 (~0.33 bps)**, **minOrderSize 0.25 base** (~75 quote), wealth **50,000 quote**, **we use ZERO leverage** (unleveraged longs can never be liquidated). **R≈0.14** (sim ~7× slower): 600 s≈71 real‑min, 5400 s≈10.7 real‑h, 10800 s≈21.4 real‑h, 86400 s≈7.1 real‑days. **`tick`/`minOrderSize`/`price` are NOT in config — read at runtime** (`_sync_precision`).
- **Current fees:** MTR≈0.52 → maker **pays** ~6 bps/leg, taker **rebate** ~3.6 bps/leg, spread ~17 bps.

**Honest ceiling:** uniform all‑positive median norm‑kappa ≈ **0.52–0.55** at sane density, so **combined ≈ 0.79·~0.53 + 0.21·(small +) ≈ 0.40–0.43** — dead‑even with the competitor's live band (combS 0.41–0.42). Budget against this, not 0.79.

## 1. Strategy in one paragraph

Warehouse a **deep, cheap, unleveraged** long buffer across as many of the 128 books as possible, then print a steady, **mostly‑positive** realized round‑trip stream — generated by whichever of two per‑book modes the fee/spread math selects: **(A) Warehouse‑maker** (the default) sells only FIFO‑oldest lots already in profit; **(B) Rebate‑harvest** (only when the taker rebate out‑earns the spread) round‑trips via taker for a guaranteed positive with no price‑move needed. Keep the **median** book positive and **≥80–96 books active**; when a book has no profitable lot and must stay active, take a **tiny bounded‑loss scratch** or let it idle in the free budget. We win on the levers the competitor leaves on the table: **breadth**, **mode‑switching to whichever fee side pays**, a **structural** (not discretionary) profit predicate, and a **bounded‑loss release** that unwinds stuck lots instead of warehousing them into the cliff.

---

## 2. The two modes (chosen PER BOOK, every step, from live fees)

A taker round‑trip crosses the spread twice but collects the taker rebate twice, so its standalone P&L ≈ **`2·taker_rebate − spread`**.

- **Mode B — REBATE‑HARVEST** when `2·taker_rebate_bps > spread_bps + HARVEST_MARGIN(2)` (i.e. rebate covers the spread, roughly `rebate > half‑spread ≈ 8.5 bps` today). Then we **buy and sell with taker continuously** — each round‑trip is guaranteed positive with **no inventory risk, no price dependence, no FIFO lockup**. This is the ideal kappa engine when the fee regime offers it. (Today's −3.6 bps rebate vs 17 bps spread ⇒ 7.2 < 17 ⇒ **harvest OFF**.)
- **Mode A — WAREHOUSE‑MAKER** otherwise (the current regime). Buy passive maker on dips; sell only FIFO‑oldest lots already in profit past both fee legs + margin; exit on the **rebated side** (taker‑sell when MTR≥0.40, maker‑sell when MTR<0.40). Needs price to rise to realize — hence the deep cheap buffer.

Modes are per‑book and can differ across books and flip over time; warehouse inventory simply carries over when a book flips to harvest.

---

## 3. Lifecycle & timing (warmup = accumulate deep, then turn on the drip)

- **Uid scoring can't start before age 1.5 sim‑h (~10.7 real‑h)** — the `min_lookback` span is auto‑satisfied from registration by the dense columns, so this is a fixed wall you cannot skip.
- **During the warmup (age 0 → ~1.2 sim‑h): PRIORITIZE ACCUMULATION.** Build the deep cheap buffer (Section 4); sell only opportunistically (when a lot is clearly profitable). There is no scoring to chase yet, so don't burn the buffer.
- **Ramp the realize drip up over ~age 1.0–1.5 sim‑h** so that by the 1.5 sim‑h wall **every active book already has ≥3 recent round‑trips** (and ≥1 within the last 600 s) → books score immediately and at full activity, not from a cold start.
- **Steady state (age > 1.5 sim‑h):** run the per‑book mode engine + the scratch/idle ladder forever; the 1:1 replenish keeps inventory, obs and activity self‑renewing for days.

---

## 4. Accumulation (Phase that dominates the warmup, then revolves)

- **Entry (Mode A):** post‑only **maker** resting bid at/below best_bid, **dip‑biased** (`mid ≤ midEWMA(α=0.05) − ACCUM_DIP_BPS(4)`), so cost basis sits below mid → frequent profitable exits. (Entry in Mode B is taker, by definition.)
- **Lot size:** `QUOTE_LOT = 0.26` base (just above the runtime min so fee‑shave can't strand a lot). Read min/tick/price at runtime.
- **Deep buffer (the v2 change — credit to the competitor's "buy first" warmup):** per‑book cap `ACCUM_TARGET_LOTS = 6` lots, **global** ceiling `GLOBAL_BASE_FRAC = 0.60` (≤ **30,000 quote** of base outstanding; ~40% stays dry powder for continued dip‑buys and the revolve). During warmup, fill toward the global ceiling, dip‑weighted (deeper on books that dip more). Once a book hits 6 lots or the global cap is hit → replenishment‑only.
- **Why deep:** more cheap lots ⇒ at almost any price *some* book has a profitable oldest lot ⇒ the realize drip rarely starves (directly answers the "no profitable book right now" problem). Cost is bounded dead capital (unleveraged, never liquidatable, score‑invisible).
- **Keep queue priority:** 2.0‑tick keep‑band re‑peg; don't churn to the back of the FIFO.

## 5. Realization & the per‑book step ladder

Each step, per book, after computing `mode`, do the **first applicable** of:

1. **REAL WINNER (both modes, Mode A primary).** If a FIFO‑oldest lot clears the guaranteed‑profit predicate and the cadence is due → sell **one oldest lot**.
   ```
   exit_side    = TAKER if MTR ≥ 0.40 else MAKER          # the rebated leg
   need_bps     = max(0,open_fee_bps) + max(0,exit_fee_bps[exit_side]) + REALIZE_MARGIN(4)   # ~21–28 bps today
   sell_ok      = best_bid ≥ oldest_px·(1 + need_bps/1e4)  # test the LIVE bid
   due          = (now − last_rt_ns ≥ REALIZE_TARGET_S=300)  OR  (now ≥ activity_deadline)
   action       = prefer post-only maker reduce @ max(ask_inside, target_px), GTT 90 s;
                  at the deadline, IOC @ best_bid ONLY if it still clears need_bps.
   fragment     = exactly one oldest lot per fire (uniform tiny positive obs).
   ```
2. **REBATE‑HARVEST (Mode B only).** If `2·taker_rebate > spread+margin` and cadence due and under the volume cap → taker‑buy 1 lot then taker‑sell it next step (or sell an existing lot via taker). Guaranteed `+ (2·rebate − spread)` per RT.
3. **FIFO‑LOCKUP RELEASE (bounded loss).** If oldest lot is underwater, `oldest_age ≥ STUCK_HOLD_S(1800)`, and it blocks `≥ LOCKUP_MIN_LOTS(2)` profitable lots behind it → IOC sell the oldest at a **capped** loss `≥ −MAX_STUCK_LOSS_BPS(15)`, **iff** `median_neg_room > 0`. Frees the whole book. (Verified kappa‑safe.)
4. **SCRATCH‑TO‑STAY‑ACTIVE (bounded loss).** If the book has **no** profitable lot, harvest is off, it's about to miss its 600 s activity window, **and** dropping it would push `active_count` below the floor → take one **tiny** RT at `≥ −SCRATCH_LOSS_BPS(4)` to keep it active. Else →
5. **IDLE (free).** Stop quoting the book; it records nothing (`kappa=None`), consuming the 48‑book free budget. Preferred over any larger loss.
6. **ACCUMULATE / REPLENISH.** Independently (composes with 1/2), if `lots < ACCUM_TARGET_LOTS` and under the global base cap and (dip or `lots < ACCUM_MIN_LOTS(1)`) and the book's `net_edge ≥ gate_min_edge` → post one passive dip‑biased maker buy. Every realized SELL arms an immediate 1:1 rebuild.
7. **RECONCILE.** Cancel stale orders; submit desired (all post‑only / GTT). ≤5 instr/book.

**Cadence:** `REALIZE_TARGET_S=300 s` (≈1 RT/book/5 min) sits safely above the 600 s activity floor and the 3‑obs/3 h gate, and near the density‑value plateau — fire faster only if the predicate freely offers it and we're under the cap. **Don't chase 720 RT** (the kappa gain is marginal, the cost is not).

## 6. Position store & guards

- **Store:** per `(validator, book)` an `_Inv{longs: deque}` of `(ts, qty, px, open_fee)`, matched oldest‑first by `_match_fifo` (reused from StableMakerV2, **byte‑identical to the validator**, so predicted realized PnL == scored). Track `oldest_px/age`, `last_rt_ns`, `rt_events`, `noise_bps`, `midEWMA`, lot count.
- **Median‑positive guard (replaces "zero negatives"):** before any bounded‑loss sell (steps 3/4), abort if it would push the fraction of active books net‑negative *this window* above `MEDIAN_NEG_FRAC(0.25)`. Targets the real −0.0156 failure mode (a *cluster* of losses moving the median), while permitting the harmless single‑book clears.
- **Breadth floor:** keep `active_books ≥ MIN_ACTIVE_BOOKS=80`, target **88–96**; `abandoned_count` hard‑capped < 48; **stop accumulating before abandons reach 40** so the rebuild bid doesn't keep buying into a correlated fall.
- **Capital:** `GLOBAL_BASE_FRAC=0.60` + per‑book 6‑lot cap ⇒ ≤30,000 quote base outstanding « 50,000; ~40% dry powder; zero leverage ⇒ never liquidatable.
- **Volume:** self‑throttle at `VOLUME_SAFETY=0.8` → 400,000/book/24 h‑sim (we run ~45–90k).
- **GC/axon:** keep inherited `_tune_gc()`. **Restart:** clock off `state.timestamp`, per‑validator `seen_ns`; stagger fleet restarts.

## 7. Multi‑uid pipeline & dereg economics

- **Single class, role/mode via env** (`AGENT_ROLE`); adding a uid is one config line.
- **Staggered rolling maturity:** register cohort members `REG_STAGGER ≈ 5400 sim‑s (~10.7 real‑h)` apart so one matures as the next warms up → always have scoring uids.
- **Desync siblings:** per‑uid jitter (`uid·2654435761` → ±8% on dip/margin/timers) so siblings don't lift each other's reduces or stack the same dip. Cross‑uid fills are *real* PnL (not self‑trades); jitter prevents cannibalization.
- **Dereg economics (NEEDS REAL NUMBERS — Open Q):** hold a uid ≥ `DEREG_MIN_EMISSION_DAYS=3` real‑days; recycle when placement decays below cohort median **and** it has banked ≥ its re‑registration cost.

## 11. Review — strengths, residual risks, open questions

**Strengths (high confidence):** the scoring mechanics are read from source and empirically replayed; the FIFO predicate makes intended realizes positive *by construction*; the two‑mode switch means we always trade the side the fee regime pays; zero leverage removes liquidation risk entirely; the bounded‑loss ladder + median guard directly target the one historical failure mode (−0.0156 from clustered losses).

**Residual risks (need live validation):**
- **Realize fill‑rate at ~21–28 bps `need_bps`** is the true binding constraint and is unmeasured — if profitable exits are rarer than assumed, density/breadth suffer. *Mitigation:* the deep buffer + dip‑biased cheap entries lower the bar; measure it in the A/B before scaling.
- **Correlated multi‑day downtrend** could still strand many books at once; the lockup‑release + "stop accumulating before 40 abandons" are the guards, but the tail is real — the trend‑stress dryrun must prove active≥80 holds.
- **Deep buffer = more dead capital** (≤30k unrealized at risk). Score‑invisible and unleveraged, but it ties up capital and is a real‑PnL drawdown; acceptable under "goal is score," not under "don't lose money."
- **Mode‑switch churn** if `2·rebate ≈ spread` oscillates around the threshold — needs the `HARVEST_MARGIN` hysteresis (and possibly a dwell timer) so books don't flip every step.

**Open questions (get real numbers before recycle/scaling):** (1) re‑registration cost + emission/top‑uid/day → dereg recycle math; (2) measured per‑book time‑to‑first‑profitable‑exit + realize fill‑rate at live fees; (3) exact `pnl_score` contribution of the thin stream; (4) does the deep buffer ever brush the global cap under a sustained dip (tune `GLOBAL_BASE_FRAC`).

### Realize predicate (corrected — net-margin, exit-side dispatcher, per book per pass)
For the oldest FIFO lot at `px0`, compute net-realized bps for each exit and pick the one that is **net ≥ REALIZE_MARGIN_BPS at the smallest required move** (= fills soonest, protects the activity floor):
- **HARVEST** if `spread < 2·taker_rebate`: taker both legs, net ≈ `2·rebate − spread > 0` with no move needed.
- **MAKER-OUT (default):** post-only reduce at `max(best_ask_inside, px0·(1 + (2·maker_fee + REALIZE_MARGIN)/1e4))`; captures the spread, fills when lifted. Net = `spread + Δbid − 2·maker_fee`. Best in WIDE-spread regimes (spread covers the fee).
- **TAKER-OUT:** hit best_bid when `best_bid ≥ px0·(1 + (maker_fee − taker_rebate + REALIZE_MARGIN)/1e4)`; immediate fill, uses the rebate. Best in TIGHT-spread regimes.
- **BOUNDED-LOSS release:** if `lot_age ≥ 180 s` and underwater, taker-out at a loss `≤ 15 bps` (age-triggered).
- **SCRATCH:** if a needed-active book has no profitable exit and is about to miss its 600 s window, one taker RT at `≤ 4 bps` loss. Else **IDLE** (free, within 48 budget).
Dispatcher order each pass: `spread<3.2 → HARVEST | bid clears maker-out or taker-out → cheaper of the two | age≥180s & underwater → bounded-loss | marginal & 600s-gap → scratch | else hold/idle`. **Re-read live per-book spread/fees each pass** (spread compressed 16.8→4.6 bps in hours today).

### HONEST feasibility (correcting the sim's optimism)
- The feasibility sim used **touch=fill + favorable noise** → its kappa (0.57–0.71) is **inflated** (perfect-branch artifact). The real number is **fill-rate-gated**.
- **Realistic target: land in the competitor's top-6 band, raw kappa ≈ 0.06–0.10 (kappa_score ~0.52, combined ~0.41).** Beating weak uid145 (0.0097) is near-certain; **matching uid101 (0.093) is the stretch goal and depends on holding the activity floor.** Do NOT promise 0.45/top-3.
- **THE binding constraint = real maker FILL-RATE → the ≥80 active-book floor.** Sim shows only ~78–84% active (≈100/128) even optimistically — **0–4 books of margin** over the 80 floor. If resting orders fill materially slower than they're touched, the floor breaks → past the 48-idle cliff → **kappa→0**.
- **Genuine failure mode = sustained correlated downtrend:** bids never clear need_bps, all lots strand underwater, activity→0. **Guards: age-triggered bounded-loss release + stop accumulating before ~40 abandons/book.** Must be validated in the dryrun and watched live.
